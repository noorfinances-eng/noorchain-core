Multi-sig Committee (3/5) Structure
Version 1.1
Last Updated: 2025-XX-XX
1. Purpose of the Multi-sig Committee

The Multi-sig Committee is the on-chain executive body of the
NOORCHAIN Foundation, responsible for:

executing governance-approved actions,

managing the Foundation Allocation (5% of total supply),

ensuring transparent and compliant handling of on-chain resources,

protecting the protocol against unilateral or unsafe actions.

It operates under a 3-out-of-5 signature threshold, following widely adopted
best practices used by major blockchain foundations.

2. Multi-sig Composition
2.1 Signers (Five Members)

Recommended structure:

Founder / Project Lead
Ensures technical and strategic continuity.

NGO / Social Impact Representative
Reflects the needs of Curators and PoSS partners.

Technical Expert
Reviews protocol upgrades and PoSS parameter changes.

Legal / Compliance Representative
Ensures alignment with Swiss Legal Light CH.

Educational / Community Representative
Represents schools, education partners, and public stakeholders.

Rotation

Members 2–5 may rotate every 12–24 months to preserve decentralization and independence.

2.2 Threshold Rule

All on-chain actions require 3-of-5 signatures.

Ensures accountability, protection against unilateral control, and operational security.

3. Responsibilities of the Multi-sig
3.1 Manage the Foundation Allocation (5%)

Authorized uses include:

audits and security research,

documentation and governance work,

NGO/school partnership support,

technical grants and bounties,

infrastructure costs necessary for network visibility and reliability.

3.2 Execute Governance Actions

Under Foundation or Board approval, the Multi-sig may:

activate or adjust PoSS parameters within allowed limits,

initiate network upgrades,

deploy approved modules or updates,

manage governance-related contracts.

3.3 Publish Transparency Reports

Frequency: at least every 6 or 12 months.

Reports must include:

actions executed,

funds spent,

PoSS parameter adjustments,

status of multi-sig membership.

4. Legal & Compliance Restrictions

The Multi-sig must operate under the following non-negotiable rules:

4.1 No Custody of User Funds

The committee may never manage or hold assets belonging to users.

4.2 No Promised Returns

The Multi-sig may not:

distribute financial yields,

offer returns,

operate staking-as-a-service,

promote speculative reward systems.

4.3 No Market Manipulation

Prohibited actions include:

price control attempts,

artificially influencing liquidity,

speculative use of Foundation funds.

4.4 Swiss Legal Light CH Compliance

Every action must remain aligned with:

non-profit purpose,

transparency obligations,

no investment offering,

no financial intermediation.

5. Explicitly Allowed Operations

The Multi-sig may execute:

Audit-related expenditures

Technical or documentation grants

NGO / school partnership funding

Infrastructure and hosting expenses

Governance parameter updates (within protocol limits)

Deployment of approved upgrades

Operational payments aligned with Foundation purpose

6. Explicitly Forbidden Operations

The Multi-sig is prohibited from:

Holding tokens belonging to third parties

Issuing loans or credit instruments

Guaranteeing yields or returns

Selling tokens as financial investments

Using Foundation funds for trading or speculation

Modifying Genesis allocations after mainnet launch

Engaging in custodial or PSP-like operations

7. Transparency Rules

The Committee must:

disclose all transactions publicly,

maintain a clear operational log,

publish periodic reviews,

update public records when signers change,

declare conflicts of interest when applicable,

document each governance or parameter action.

8. Replacement & Rotation
8.1 Replacement

A signer may be replaced in cases of:

resignation,

inactivity,

violation of ethical principles,

conflict of interest,

governance decision requiring reconfiguration.

8.2 Rotation

To ensure decentralization, members other than the Founder should rotate every 12–24 months.
This supports independence, continuity, and resilience.

9. Technical Implementation Notes

Implemented as a Cosmos SDK multi-sig account (Ethermint-compatible).

Signing keys will be generated in Phase 7 (Mainnet Preparation).

Foundation must maintain:

multisig.json configuration

list of active and historical signers

public key registry for transparency

10. Adoption

This Multi-sig Committee structure is formally adopted by the
NOORCHAIN Foundation Board as part of Phase 5.
